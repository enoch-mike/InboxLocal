7 Restaurant Email Newsletter Ideas That Bring Customers Back

June 14, 2026 • 5 min read • Restaurants

Running a restaurant is hard. Between managing staff, inventory, and the daily rush, marketing often falls by the wayside.

But here's the thing: 72% of diners prefer email for hearing about restaurant specials (Toast Restaurant Survey). Your customers actually want to hear from you — they just need a reason to come back.

Here are 7 newsletter ideas that work, with real subject lines you can steal.

1 The Dish of the Week

Feature one standout dish with a beautiful photo and a short story behind it. This works because it's visual, timely, and creates urgency.

Subject line: Chef Marco's new spring pasta 🍝
CTA: Reserve a Table

2 Limited-Time Offer

Scarcity drives action. Whether it's a happy hour expansion or a weekend special, give customers a reason to visit now.

Subject line: 🍸 Now 4-7pm — our new happy hour just got longer
Offer: Half-price apps, 4-7pm weekdays

3 Behind the Scenes

Customers love feeling like insiders. Share a photo of your team prepping, a story about where ingredients come from, or a peek into the kitchen.

Subject line: We're teaming up with Oak Hill Farm 🌱
Why it works: Local partnerships build community trust

4 Seasonal Menu Launch

Every season is an excuse to refresh your menu. Build anticipation before the launch and drive traffic opening week.

Subject line: Fall has arrived on your plate 🍂
Best time: Send the Tuesday before the menu change

5 Event Announcement

Trivia nights, live music, wine tastings — events fill seats on otherwise slow nights. Promote them in your newsletter.

Subject line: Trivia Night is back! Every Tuesday at 7PM 🧠
Pro tip: Include a prize to drive attendance

6 Customer Spotlight

Feature a regular customer or a great review. Social proof is one of the most powerful marketing tools.

Subject line: Meet Sarah — she's eaten here 40 times this year 🎉
Why it works: Makes others want to be featured too

7 The "We Miss You" Re-engagement

If a customer hasn't visited in 60+ days, send a personalized "we miss you" email with a small incentive to return.

Subject line: It's been too long, [Name] — here's a drink on us 🥂
Offer: Free appetizer with entree purchase

How Often Should You Send?

For restaurants, once per week is the sweet spot. Tuesday or Wednesday morning (10am) gets the highest open rates. Thursday evening also works well for weekend reservation reminders.
