Email Marketing for Gyms: 7 Ways to Keep Members Coming Back

June 14, 2026 • 6 min read • Fitness

Gym membership retention is one of the toughest challenges in fitness. The January rush fades, February brings drop-offs, and by March, many gyms have lost 30-40% of their new members.

The solution? Email marketing for gyms isn't just about sending class schedules — it's about building a community that members don't want to leave.

Here are 7 proven ways to use email marketing to retain gym members and keep your classes full.

1 Member Transformation Stories

Nothing inspires action like real results. Feature a member of the month with their before/after photos, their story, and a short testimonial. These emails consistently see the highest engagement in fitness newsletters — up to 18% higher CTR than promotional emails.

Subject line: Meet Sarah — she crushed her 90-day goal 💪
CTA: Book a Free Trial
Why it works: Social proof makes others want to achieve similar results

2 Weekly Workout Tip + Video Demo

Give members a reason to open every email. Each week, share one actionable tip with a short video demo (15-30 seconds). It positions your trainers as experts and gives subscribers content they can actually use.

Subject line: Fix your squat in 30 seconds 🏋️
Content: Quick form tip with embedded video
Bonus: Include a "try this at home" version for days they can't make it to the gym

3 Class Schedule Highlights

Don't just list every class — highlight the ones with limited availability. Scarcity drives action. Mention when a popular class has only 3-5 spots remaining. This creates urgency and helps fill less popular time slots.

Subject line: Only 3 spots left in tonight's HIIT class 🔥
Secondary CTA: "Book Your Spot" — direct link to booking page

4 Nutrition & Wellness Tips

Email marketing for gyms works best when it goes beyond workouts. Members want holistic wellness advice. Share a healthy recipe, hydration tips, or recovery strategies. This positions your gym as a comprehensive health resource, not just a place to lift weights.

Subject line: Post-workout meal prep in 15 minutes 🥗
Content: Quick recipe with photo + nutrition breakdown

5 Referral Challenge Campaigns

Referral programs have 31% higher conversion in fitness than other verticals. Run a monthly challenge where existing members earn rewards for bringing friends. Email is the perfect channel to promote this — segment your most engaged members and invite them personally.

Subject line: Bring a friend — both get 2 weeks free 💪
Offer: Referral discount + prize for top referrer each month

6 New Class or Equipment Announcements

Keep members excited about what's new. Whether it's a new spin bike, a yoga instructor, or a Saturday morning HIIT class, make announcements feel like events. Include a photo and a teaser video.

Subject line: 🔥 NEW: Saturday morning HIIT is here
CTA: "Book First Class Free"

7 The "We Miss You" Re-engagement Sequence

When a member hasn't visited in 14+ days, send an automated sequence. Start with a friendly check-in, add a motivational story by day 21, and include a small incentive by day 30. This alone can recover 15-20% of at-risk members.

Subject line (Day 14): Haven't seen you in a while, [Name] 👋
Subject line (Day 30): Come back this week — your next class is on us 🎁

How Often Should You Send?

For gyms and fitness studios, once per week is ideal. Wednesday morning (10AM local time) gets the highest open rates — it's midweek motivation for members who might be thinking about skipping their workout.
